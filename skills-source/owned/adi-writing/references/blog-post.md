# Adi Writing Mode — Blog Post

Use for:
- essays
- blog posts
- reflective public writing
- technical explainers with room to breathe

## Default shape
- open with the actual point, tension, or observation
- explain why it matters
- use examples, anecdotes, or concrete framing
- end with a takeaway, lesson, or direction

## What usually works
- a sharp opening claim in the first paragraph
- strong section titles
- memorable framing devices
- personal experience tied to useful insight
- a practical takeaway even when the piece is reflective
- clarity over polish

## Opening patterns that fit
- "Here is the thing I noticed."
- "One thing people get wrong is..."
- "A side effect of X is Y, and I had been missing that."
- "I tried something specific, and it changed how I think about..."

## Endings that fit
- a clean takeaway
- a short reflection that lands the point
- a recommendation or lesson
- a brief forward-looking note when it feels earned

## Watchouts
- do not make the opening too slow
- do not over-explain once the point is clear
- do not start with greetings or throat-clearing
- do not narrate the structure of the post before making the point
- do not add subscribe blocks, quote-of-the-day sections, or sign-off formulas by default
- do not stretch the ending after the main idea is already done

## Quick do/don't contrast
- Do: start with the live idea.
- Don't: spend a paragraph welcoming the reader before the point appears.
- Do: use personal material when it gives the insight weight.
- Don't: drift into diary mode without giving the reader something to hold onto.
- Do: keep the voice conversational and specific.
- Don't: polish it into generic "founder content."
