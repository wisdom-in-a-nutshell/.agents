# Adi Writing Mode — Email

Use for:
- professional emails
- personal emails
- client or collaborator outreach
- thoughtful replies

## Default shape
- state the purpose early
- be polite but not bloated
- keep the body easy to scan
- end with a clear next step or ask

## What usually works
- direct opening
- compact paragraphs
- clear ask, recommendation, or answer
- human warmth without fake politeness
- a simple sign-off when one is needed

## Watchouts
- do not sound like a corporate account manager
- do not over-apologize
- do not add unnecessary filler before the point
- do not default to "hope you are doing well" unless it is sincere and specific
- do not bury the actual ask in the last paragraph

## Practical guidance
- For professional mail: open with the purpose, then the recommendation or ask.
- For collaborator mail: keep it warm, direct, and action-oriented.
- For personal replies: let the warmth be specific, not formulaic.
- If a two-line email works, prefer that over a polished five-paragraph version.
